Thank you for supporting our #CbK300 crowdfunding campaign!

We’re turning community spaces into technology education centers for Philadelphia’s youth. We are working to ensure that regardless of who they are, where they come from, and what resources they have, all Philadelphia youth have access to the technology-driven education, resources, and support that prepares them for success in the innovation economy.

We’ve successfully run programs for three years, reaching almost 100 students every week in their communities and schools. We have the vision and plan in place to reach more students and have a more significant impact today, if we raise the funding to support our goals.

The ABLE Center is a Youth and Community Support Center. ABLE is short for Abundant Life Empowerment Missions and we are an affiliate of Eddie’s House. Our mission is to “empower lives”. We currently run several programs for ages 7-18 and the community at large. We would like to enhance and expand our services/facility to provide and prepare young people for their future, as well as expose them to the possibilities of their future by teaching kids how to code.

Our school's mission is to inspire young minds to dream big! Our students have participated in the Hour of Code every year since it's inception. There is a very high interest at all levels, kindergarten through fifth grade, in coding. We have an enrichment class that competed in the category of coding last year at the Philly Technology Exposition and Competition.

PEC’S Center for Digital Inclusion and Technology continues to identify innovative programs to support our after school youth. Coding is an essential skillset that will prepare our students well in STEM careers. We are excited to participate in this collaboration with Coded by Kids.

Adaire is committed to a liberal arts educational approach. And we know that growing and supporting the studies of STEM are a big part of education success. The Mighty Writers mission is to teach kids to think and communicate clearly and write with clarity. Writing is based on language. We just opened up a bilingual Spanish-speaking site, why wouldn't we add coding as another language?

Penn Treaty School strives to ignite students' passion for civic participation and personal development by cultivating college and career preparation (AVID), providing opportunities for authentic experiences in the fields of science and technology (STEM Academy), and promoting a culture of respect and responsibility.

Our Team

Sylvester Mobley
Operations Manager

Driven by a desire to increase both access to tech education and diversity in the tech industry, Sylvester Mobley founded Coded by Kids in 2013. Through word of mouth, the program grew in popularity and he expanded his focus to incorporate adult education with the founding of Coded by U in 2015. Sylvester works to use technology education to fight the conditions that contribute to systemic poverty, such as the lack of meaningful employment opportunities in many communities.

Maggie Deptola
Operations Manager

Maggie joined the Coded by Kids team eager to contribute her energy, skills, and sense of humor to an organization with a positive and impactful mission. Since moving to Philadelphia in 2008, Maggie has spent a lot of time getting to know Philadelphia’s diverse communities as both a student and resident. She has experienced firsthand how this diversity is not reflected in the tech scene, and is a strong advocate for using education as a catalyst for social change.

Michaela Ochnich
Chief Technology Officer

Michaela first joined Coded by Kids as a volunteer teacher looking for ways to augment her Computer Science classes with real world experiences. Frustrated by her own experience with the disconnect between skills taught in university classrooms and skills necessary to have a successful career in tech, she is passionate about ensuring students have hands-on experience with the development process.

Danae Mobley
Chief Experience Officer

Danae believes that all people deserve an equitable quality of life and environment despite their circumstance. She brings this passion for equity to her role as the Chief Learning Officer for Coded by Kids and is committed to fostering a culture of engagement and enthusiasm in order to build the next generation of innovators.

Ross Coley
Program Support Coordinator

Ross first came to Coded by Kids as a student interested in seeing what other opportunities were available to him beyond the traditional track of going to college and getting a degree. With Sylvester’s help, he learned how to code and was soon good enough to teach younger students at the weekly rec center classes. He uses his personal experience with learning to code to influence his teaching methods.
